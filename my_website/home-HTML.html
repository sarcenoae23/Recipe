OUR FAVORITE FOOD DISH

  • Crispy Tofu Sisig
    • Tofu Sisig is a dish composed of deep fried tofu cubes, chopped chili peppers, and onion. It is healthier alternative to traditional pork sisig because it does not use any meat and greasy ingredients. Aside from being healthy, this crispy tofu sisig recipe is also vegan.
    A&J: Tofu sisig has become one of my favorites because a friend cooked it for me, and I was amazed by how delicious it turned out.


    Here's the preview of dish :>

RECIPE


Ingredients
Quantity
Block of firm tofu 6
Red Onion 3 pcs
Green chili pepper 1 pcs
30 grams of Small oyster sauce 30 grams 2 pcs
Soy Sauce 2 tablespoon
Calamansi Juice/Lime Juice 1 Tablespoon
80 ml of Mayonnaise 1 pcs
Salt 1 teaspoon
Pepper 1 teaspoon
Cooking Oil 1 teaspoon
Procedures:
  1. Start by chopping 6 firm blocks of tofu into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Heat a cooking pan with oil and fry the tofu until it's golden brown. Once cooked, set the tofu aside.
  3. Next, chop 3 pieces of onion and the green chili pepper.
  4. In the same pan, add the chopped onion and green chili, and cook them together with the tofu.
  5. After cooking for 1 minute, add the oyster sauce and pepper, pinch of salt and 2 tablespoon of soy sauce.
  6. Allow it to cook for an additional minute.
  7. Finally, season the dish by adding, mayonnaise, and one tablespoon of calamansi juice.
  • Beef Steak
    • A beefsteak, often called just steak, is a flat cut of beef with parallel faces, usually cut perpendicular to the muscle fibers.
    A&J:Beef steak, which my dad always cooks, is a dish I indulge in whenever we have it.


    Here's the preview of dish :>

RECIPE


Ingredients
Quantity
Grams beef sirloin thinly sliced and cut into pieces 500 Grams
fresh calamansi juice 1/4 Cup
Soy Sauce 1 Cup
Water 4 Cups
Cooking Oil 2 Tablespoon
Procedures:
  1. In a bowl, combine beef, soy sauce, and calamansi juice. .
  2. Cover and set aside at least one hour up to overnight.
  3. Next, chop 3 pieces of onion and the green chili pepper. In a saute pan over high heat, heat enough oil to cover the bottom. Add onions and saute, stirring, until just softened.
  4. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. In the same pan, add sliced beef. Add water. Bring to a boil then simmer until tender.
  6. Remove any scum that rises to the surface. Add more water as needed until beef is tender.
  7. Once beef is tender, simmer the sauce until reduced and flavorful. Season with a little ground black pepper if desired.
  8. Return onions to the pan and cook until just softened. Serve while hot.


  • Paksiw na Bangus
    • This Paksiw na Bangus Recipe is simply fish cooked in vinegar and garlic. This seafood dish is best eaten with steamed rice.
    A&J:Paksiw na isda is one of my favorites because my mother prepares it, and it's incredibly delicious.


    Here's the preview of dish :>

RECIPE


Ingredients
Quantity
Vinegar 1/2 Cup
Water 1 Cup
Salt 2 Teaspoon
Peppercorns 1 Teaspoon
milkfish (bangus) cleaned and sliced into 4 1 medium
ginger sliced and pounded 1 knob
Garlic skin removed 6 Cloves
Onion sliced 1 Medium
finger chilies (siling pangsigang) 3 pcs
Procedures:
  1. Heat a medium-sized pan and cooking pot then add vinegar and water.
  2. Add salt and whole peppercorn then stir. Bring to a boil.
  3. Arrange the fish in the pan and add the ginger, garlic, onion, and finger chili. Cover and simmer over medium heat for 20 minutes. Serve hot with steamed rice.


  • Ginataang Tilapia
    • Ginataang tilapia is fish with spinach cooked in coconut milk.
    A&J:Ginataang tilapia is my mother's favorite dish, and when I tasted it, I was pleasantly surprised by how good it was


    Here's the preview of dish :>

RECIPE


Ingredients
Quantity
Tilapia 1 Kilo
Pechay 2 bunches
Coconut milk 2 Cups
Garlic 3 cloves
Onion 1 medium
Tomatoes 1 medium
Ginger 6 Cloves
Onion sliced 1 Thumb
Salt 1 teaspoon
Pepper 1 teaspoon
Fish Sauce 2 tablespoon
Cooking Oil 1 teaspoon
Long Green Pepper 2 pcs

Procedures:

  1. Wash and prepare the tilapia. Remove the fins, gills and gut. Make 2-3 slits on both side of the fish's body. Season both sides with salt and pepper and rub them generously. Do these steps with the other tilapia.
  2. Heat some oil in a pan. Place the tilapia gently and let it cook for a few minutes. Do not move the fish around or flip too early.
  3. Once golden and crispy, flip the fish to cook the other side as well. Transfer the fish to a strainer or paper towel to drain excess oil. Set aside.
  4. Chop and prepare the aromatics. Reduce the oil from the pan and then add the ginger, garlic and onions. Saute them for a few minutes until light brown. Add the tomatoes and crush them gently while sauteing to release the liquid.
  5. Pour the coconut milk and let it boil using low heat. Season with some pepper and fish sauce. Let this simmer for a few minutes.
  6. Add the pechay leaves and submerge them in the coconut sauce. Let them cook until soft. Add the long green chilies and simmer for another minute.Then, add the fried tilapia back to the pan. Flip and baste them with the sauce to absorb the creaminess.
  7. After a few minutes, turn the heat off. Transfer the ginataang tilapia with pechay to a serving plate.

RECIPE


Ingredients
Quantity
Pork belly, diced 1/2 lbs
Thin Coconut Milk 2 Cups
Fresh alamang (shrimp paste) 2 Tablespoon
Thai chili pepper, stemmed and minced 10 pcs
long chili (siling haba, siling panigang), sliced 6 pcs
garlic, minced 3 cloves
Onion, minced 1 medium
Thick coconut cream 1 cup
Salt 1 teaspoon
Pepper 1 teaspoon
Ginger Minced 3 tablespoon
Procedures:
  1. Heat a pan and then pour-in the cooking oil.
  2. Sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger.
  3. Add the pork and cook for 8 minutes or until the color becomes light brown.
  4. Mix then coconut milk and alamang (shrimp paste).
  5. Bring to a boil, lower heat and then simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Add chili peppers and cook until half the liquid has evaporated.
  7. Add the long chili (siling haba/panigang).
  8. Pour in thick coconut cream and continue cooking until oil comes out from the cream.
  9. Add salt and ground black pepper to taste.
  10. Serve hot.
